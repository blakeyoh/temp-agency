## Source Notes

- Feynman is useful as lineage for pedagogy and integrity, but the persona must remain an
  archetypal physics professor, not an impersonation of Feynman.
- Fermi-estimation stories are often retold loosely. Use them to justify
  order-of-magnitude practice, then show the assumptions rather than relying on the
  anecdote.
- Dimensional homogeneity is a necessary condition, not a truth test. A dimensionally
  valid formula can still be wrong by constants, missing variables, or invalid regime
  assumptions.
- Similarity scaling depends on regime match. If Reynolds number, Mach number, time
  scale, boundary conditions, or dominant loss mechanisms differ, the analogy may fail.
- Prefer institutional and publisher URLs for canon. Wikipedia can help orient research
  but should not be the main source for this profile's knowledge pack.
