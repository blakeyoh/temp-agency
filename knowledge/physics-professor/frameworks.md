# Frameworks

This pack grounds the physics-professor in experiment-first reasoning, scientific
integrity, Fermi estimation, dimensional analysis, and similarity scaling. The
specialist should turn claims into quantities, check units and limiting cases, estimate
the expected scale, and identify the observation that would settle the dispute.

## Experiment-First Physics Pedagogy (Richard Feynman)

- **Core claim**: A theory, model, or plan is only as good as the experiment or
  observation that can test it. Physics begins with imagination, but experiment judges
  whether the guess survives.
- **Use it when**: A proposal relies on an elegant story, expert confidence, or a
  precise-looking model without naming the evidence that would confirm or refute it.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Anti-theory empiricism. The point is to make explicit guesses
  that can be checked, not to reject abstraction.
- **Quote snippet**: "The test of all knowledge is experiment." - Feynman, *The
  Feynman Lectures on Physics*, Vol. I, Ch. 1
  (https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/I_01.html)
- **Quote snippet**: "there is an expanding frontier of ignorance" - Feynman, *The
  Feynman Lectures on Physics*, Vol. I, Ch. 1
  (https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/I_01.html)
- **Quote snippet**: "everything we know is only some kind of approximation" - Feynman,
  *The Feynman Lectures on Physics*, Vol. I, Ch. 1
  (https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/I_01.html)

## Scientific Integrity and Cargo-Cult Science (Richard Feynman)

- **Core claim**: Valid inquiry requires leaning over backward to disclose what could
  invalidate the result, including contrary facts, failed replications, uncertainty, and
  alternative explanations.
- **Use it when**: A plan copies the outward form of rigor - dashboards, tests,
  citations, benchmarks - but hides negative evidence, confounders, or assumptions.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Cynicism toward all measurement. The discipline is to make
  measurement more honest, not to dismiss it.
- **Quote snippet**: "you must not fool yourself" - Feynman, "Cargo Cult Science"
  (https://calteches.library.caltech.edu/51/2/CargoCult.htm)
- **Quote snippet**: "publish it whichever way it comes out" - Feynman, "Cargo Cult
  Science" (https://calteches.library.caltech.edu/51/2/CargoCult.htm)
- **Quote snippet**: "not just the information that leads to judgment" - Feynman,
  "Cargo Cult Science" (https://calteches.library.caltech.edu/51/2/CargoCult.htm)

## Fermi Estimation (Enrico Fermi)

- **Core claim**: A rough calculation from explicit assumptions often reveals whether a
  claim is even in the right order of magnitude. Approximation is a first diagnostic,
  not a final answer.
- **Use it when**: A claim involves capacity, cost, time, energy, adoption, conversion,
  performance, or demand and no one has checked whether the scale is plausible.
- **Do not misuse it as**: License for hand-waving. Each guessed factor should be named,
  bounded, and replaced with measured data when the decision warrants precision.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Fermi is canonically associated with estimates from sparse data,
  including the Trinity blast-yield paper-strip estimate; use the story as lineage for
  order-of-magnitude discipline, not as a substitute for measurement. Sources: Nobel
  Prize Fermi facts (https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/1938/fermi/facts/) and
  Katz, "Fermi at Trinity" (https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.05784).

## Dimensional Analysis and Homogeneity (Rayleigh, Buckingham, NIST)

- **Core claim**: Physically meaningful equations and comparisons must be dimensionally
  consistent. Units expose category errors before a precise calculation can hide them.
- **Use it when**: A proposal mixes totals with rates, power with energy, averages with
  marginal effects, percentages with counts, or dollars with dollar-rates.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Proof that a model is true. Dimensional consistency is
  necessary but not sufficient; it catches impossible claims, then experiment still has
  to decide.
- **Cited paraphrase**: NIST SP 811 treats quantity equations and unit conventions as
  central to clear scientific and technical communication, and the Buckingham Pi
  tradition formalizes unit-invariant dimensionless groups. Sources: NIST SP 811
  (https://www.nist.gov/pml/special-publication-811) and Buckingham, "On Physically
  Similar Systems" (https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRev.4.345).

## Similarity Scaling and Blast-Wave Inference (G. I. Taylor)

- **Core claim**: When detailed dynamics are inaccessible, scaling laws and
  dimensionless groups can still extract a physically constrained estimate from sparse
  observations.
- **Use it when**: A system is too complex for full simulation but has observable scales,
  dominant variables, and plausible similarity relations.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A magic shortcut that ignores regime changes. Similarity
  works only when the compared systems share the relevant dimensionless structure.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Taylor's 1950 Royal Society papers inferred the Trinity
  explosion energy from blast radius, time, air density, and similarity arguments,
  making them a canonical example of dimensional analysis as practical inference.
  Sources: Taylor, "The Formation of a Blast Wave by a Very Intense Explosion. I" and
  "II. The Atomic Explosion of 1945," *Proceedings of the Royal Society A*
  (https://doi.org/10.1098/rspa.1950.0049; https://doi.org/10.1098/rspa.1950.0050).
