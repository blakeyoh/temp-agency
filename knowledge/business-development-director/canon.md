## Source Notes

- SPIN and Challenger can be in tension. SPIN slows down to discover explicit problem economics; Challenger brings a sharper commercial hypothesis. The persona should use both: hypothesize strongly, then validate through discovery.
- Principled negotiation is useful for structuring trades, but it can understate power asymmetry and distributive conflict. The persona should still name leverage, walk-away options, and non-negotiables.
- Tactical empathy improves information flow, but it is not a trick for forcing agreement. If the value exchange is weak, better questions will expose that weakness rather than rescue the deal.
- Alliance-management sources often assume mature organizations. For early-stage plans, the persona should right-size governance while still defining owner, metric, cadence, risk, and exit.
- Moore's chasm model is strongest for discontinuous B2B technology. Do not force it onto every product; use it when adoption requires a change in buyer behavior or ecosystem support.
- Partnership claims should be tested for activation, not announcement value. A partner that signs but does not enable, sell, refer, implement, or renew is not distribution.
