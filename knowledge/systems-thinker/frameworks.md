# Frameworks

This pack grounds the systems-thinker in system dynamics, leverage-point analysis,
organizational learning, and system archetypes. The specialist should move from events
to structure before recommending action.

## System Dynamics (Jay Forrester)

- **Core claim**: Behavior emerges from accumulations, flows, feedback, and delays,
  often in ways that defeat linear intuition.
- **Use it when**: A problem recurs after fixes, grows worse after success, or reacts
  slowly enough that the causal link is hidden.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A license to make every problem too abstract to act on.
- **Quote snippet**: "Counterintuitive. That's Forrester's word" — Donella Meadows,
  "Leverage Points" (https://donellameadows.org/archives/leverage-points-places-to-intervene-in-a-system/)

## Leverage Points (Donella Meadows)

- **Core claim**: System interventions differ radically in power; parameters are weak,
  while rules, goals, paradigms, and the capacity to transcend paradigms are stronger.
- **Use it when**: A plan proposes changing numbers, incentives, dashboards, headcount,
  or process without asking what goal the system is organized to produce.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A checklist that always requires paradigm change; low-leverage
  moves can still matter tactically.
- **Quote snippet**: "Leverage points are points of power." — Meadows, "Leverage Points"
  (https://donellameadows.org/archives/leverage-points-places-to-intervene-in-a-system/)

## Learning Organization (Peter Senge)

- **Core claim**: Organizations repeat failures when they cannot see the structures and
  mental models that generate their behavior.
- **Use it when**: A team treats training, policy reminders, or heroic effort as the
  solution to a structural pattern.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Generic culture talk detached from observable feedback loops.
- **Quote snippet**: "Today's problems come from yesterday's solutions." — Peter Senge,
  *The Fifth Discipline*, 1990, p. 57

## Systems Archetypes (Senge / Braun)

- **Core claim**: Recurring structures such as Fixes That Fail, Shifting the Burden,
  Limits to Growth, and Tragedy of the Commons predict the next failure mode.
- **Use it when**: A story sounds unique but the structure resembles a known pattern.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A label that replaces causal mapping.
- **Quote snippet**: "The essence of the discipline of systems thinking lies in a shift
  of mind" — Peter Senge, *The Fifth Discipline*, 1990, p. 68
