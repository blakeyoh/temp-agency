# Canon

| Source | Citation | Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| "Leverage Points: Places to Intervene in a System" | Donella Meadows, 1999, https://donellameadows.org/archives/leverage-points-places-to-intervene-in-a-system/ | Primary operational hierarchy for evaluating proposed interventions. |
| "Dancing with Systems" | Donella Meadows, 2001, https://donellameadows.org/archives/dancing-with-systems/ | Adds humility, experimentation, and responsiveness; prevents systems thinking from becoming rigid grand theory. |
| *Thinking in Systems* | Donella Meadows, Chelsea Green, 2008 | Canonical vocabulary for stocks, flows, feedback loops, delays, resilience, and bounded rationality in systems. |
| *Industrial Dynamics* | Jay W. Forrester, MIT Press, 1961 | Foundational system dynamics text; anchors the profile's insistence that structure drives behavior. |
| "Counterintuitive Behavior of Social Systems" | Jay W. Forrester, 1971; available via MIT/System Dynamics Society references | Source for the claim that social systems often respond opposite to intuitive policy interventions. |
| *The Fifth Discipline* | Peter Senge, Doubleday, 1990 | Connects systems thinking to organizational learning and mental models. |
| "The Systems Archetypes" | William Braun, 2002, https://www.albany.edu/faculty/gpr/PAD724/724WebArticles/sys_archetypes.pdf | Compact teaching reference for archetypes used in profile methodology. |

## Source Notes

- Meadows' leverage-point list is an "invitation to think more broadly," not a recipe.
  The profile should rank leverage, then still recommend a concrete next move.
- Forrester-style modeling is strongest when the issue has accumulations, feedback, and
  delay; do not force it onto simple one-step causal questions.
- Senge's mental-model language is useful only when paired with structural evidence.
