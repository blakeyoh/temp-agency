# Frameworks

This pack grounds the military three-star-general in operational command: converting intent into executable action across time, space, resources, authority, and uncertainty. The specialist should pressure plans for friction, logistics, command relationships, tempo, branches, sequels, reserves, and decision cycles before recommending action.

## Clausewitzian Friction and Uncertainty (Carl von Clausewitz)

- **Core claim**: Plans meet resistance from uncertainty, human limits, incomplete information, chance, fear, and small accumulated difficulties. Execution is never the paper plan.
- **Use it when**: A proposal assumes clean handoffs, stable information, predictable actors, or uninterrupted execution.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Fatalism. Friction is a reason to prepare branches, reserves, rehearsals, and clear intent, not a reason to avoid action.
- **Quote snippet**: "Everything is very simple in War, but the simplest thing is difficult." - Clausewitz, *On War*, Book I, Chapter VII (https://www.gutenberg.org/files/1946/1946-h/1946-h.htm)
- **Quote snippet**: "War is the province of uncertainty" - Clausewitz, *On War*, Book I, Chapter III (https://www.gutenberg.org/files/1946/1946-h/1946-h.htm)

## Mission Command and Commander's Intent (U.S. Army ADP 5-0 / ADP 6-0)

- **Core claim**: Commanders give purpose, end state, key tasks, resources, and risk boundaries so subordinates can act when the original plan no longer fits.
- **Use it when**: Teams need decentralized execution, fast adaptation, or clear authority across several owners.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Abdication. Mission command requires shared understanding, competence, resources, and clear decision rights.
- **Quote snippet**: "Mission command requires an environment of trust and shared understanding" - ADP 5-0, para. 1-13 (https://armypubs.army.mil/epubs/DR_pubs/DR_a/ARN18126-ADP_5-0-000-WEB-3.pdf)
- **Quote snippet**: "The more concise the commander's intent, the easier it is to understand and recall." - ADP 5-0, para. 1-38 (https://armypubs.army.mil/epubs/DR_pubs/DR_a/ARN18126-ADP_5-0-000-WEB-3.pdf)

## Operational Art and Branches/Sequels (U.S. Army ADP 5-0)

- **Core claim**: Operational command links tactical action to strategic purpose through end state, sequencing, resources, decision points, branches, and sequels.
- **Use it when**: A plan spans multiple teams, phases, or outcomes, especially when success, stalemate, or failure each require different next actions.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Bureaucratic planning theater. The point is freedom of action, not a larger slide deck.
- **Quote snippet**: "Branches anticipate situations that require changes to the basic plan." - ADP 5-0, para. 2-39 (https://armypubs.army.mil/epubs/DR_pubs/DR_a/ARN18126-ADP_5-0-000-WEB-3.pdf)
- **Quote snippet**: "Sequels are based on outcomes of current operations" - ADP 5-0, para. 2-40 (https://armypubs.army.mil/epubs/DR_pubs/DR_a/ARN18126-ADP_5-0-000-WEB-3.pdf)

## Maneuver Warfare, Tempo, and Decentralization (USMC MCDP 1)

- **Core claim**: In a competitive, uncertain environment, speed and focus generate advantage only when paired with decentralization, initiative, and adaptation.
- **Use it when**: A team faces a live opponent, rapidly changing market, incident, negotiation, or execution window where delay loses options.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Speed worship. Tempo without focus and shared understanding creates disorder inside your own force.
- **Quote snippet**: "speed is a weapon" - USMC MCDP 1, *Warfighting* (https://www.marines.mil/Portals/1/Publications/MCDP%201%20Warfighting.pdf)
- **Quote snippet**: "Efforts to fully centralize military operations... are inconsistent" - USMC MCDP 1, *Warfighting* (https://www.marines.mil/Portals/1/Publications/MCDP%201%20Warfighting.pdf)

## OODA and Decision-Cycle Competition (John Boyd)

- **Core claim**: Advantage comes from observing, orienting, deciding, and acting in a way that adapts faster and more coherently than the opposing system.
- **Use it when**: The plan depends on learning under pressure, disrupting an opponent, or making timely decisions with incomplete data.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A generic "move fast" slogan. Orientation and feedback quality matter more than raw cycle speed.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Boyd's OODA work is commonly summarized as observe, orient, decide, act, with the aim of disrupting the opponent's decision cycle; see the John Boyd bibliography and OODA summary in "OODA loop" (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OODA_loop) and the source listing for *The Essence of Winning and Losing*.

## Shared Consciousness and Empowered Execution (Stanley McChrystal)

- **Core claim**: Complex networked work requires broad shared information plus decentralized authority; otherwise local teams cannot adapt quickly enough.
- **Use it when**: A plan crosses many functions, contains fast feedback, or requires teams closest to the problem to act without waiting for senior approval.
- **Do not misuse it as**: More meetings. Shared consciousness is not status theater; it is the information architecture that makes distributed execution coherent.
- **Cited paraphrase**: McChrystal argues for broad information-sharing paired with decentralized decision rights so those closest to the problem can act in real time; see "How the Military Can Teach Us to Adapt" (https://time.com/3904177/mcchrystal-team-of-teams/).
