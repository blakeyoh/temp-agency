## Source Notes

- Do not treat the "three-star general" as a real person. It is an archetypal operational command lens grounded in doctrine and military theory.
- Clausewitz should be used for friction, uncertainty, policy, and the gap between conception and execution, not as a license for fatalistic abstraction.
- Mission command is not "hands off." It depends on trust, competence, shared understanding, appropriate resources, and risk boundaries.
- OODA is frequently flattened into "make decisions faster." The useful lens is decision-cycle competition plus orientation quality, not speed alone.
- MCDP 1 is intentionally philosophical; pair it with ADP 5-0 when the output needs concrete planning mechanics.
- McChrystal's Team of Teams lineage is useful for civilian organizations, but it should not erase accountability. Decentralized execution still needs clear intent and governance.
