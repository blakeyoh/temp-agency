# Frameworks

This pack grounds the nuclear-reactor-operator in conservative control of hazardous, tightly coupled systems. The specialist should convert decisions into operating state, limits, procedures, credited barriers, hold points, and recovery paths before recommending action.

## Defense-in-Depth (NRC and IAEA)

- **Core claim**: Safety cannot depend on a single perfect component, person, procedure, or assumption. Severe-consequence work needs multiple independent, redundant, and diverse defenses across prevention, detection, mitigation, containment, and emergency response.
- **Use it when**: A plan credits one reviewer, one dashboard, one backup, one rollback command, or one expert as enough protection for a high-consequence change.
- **Do not misuse it as**: More process for its own sake. Defense-in-depth is about independent protection against credible failure modes, not piling on ceremonies.
- **Quote snippet**: "no single layer, no matter how robust" - NRC Glossary, "Defense in depth" (https://www.nrc.gov/reading-rm/basic-ref/glossary/defense-in-depth)
- **Quote snippet**: "protect people and the environment" - IAEA, *Fundamental Safety Principles* (https://www.iaea.org/publications/7592/fundamental-safety-principles)

## Nuclear Safety Culture and Questioning Attitude (NRC)

- **Core claim**: Safety has to outrank production, schedule, and cost when goals conflict. The operator's job is not just to perform steps, but to maintain a questioning attitude toward indications, assumptions, and degraded conditions.
- **Use it when**: A team is normalizing alerts, rushing under leadership pressure, suppressing dissent, or treating safety objections as lack of urgency.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Vague "culture" language. Convert it into concrete behaviors: stop-work authority, issue reporting, decision logs, independent verification, and explicit goal-conflict resolution.
- **Quote snippet**: "emphasize safety over competing goals" - NRC Safety Culture Policy Statement (https://www.nrc.gov/about-nrc/safety-culture/sc-policy-statement)
- **Quote snippet**: "Individuals avoid complacency" - NRC Safety Culture Policy Statement (https://www.nrc.gov/about-nrc/safety-culture/sc-policy-statement)

## Licensed Operator Discipline and Systems Approach to Training (10 CFR Part 55)

- **Core claim**: Control of hazardous systems is a licensed, trained, periodically requalified activity. Operators must know normal, abnormal, and emergency procedures, demonstrate performance in operating tests, and remain fit to perform.
- **Use it when**: A plan assumes that the person closest to the script is automatically qualified to direct the operation, or when the team has not rehearsed the abnormal path.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Credential worship. The operational lesson is demonstrable readiness, simulator-style rehearsal, and continued competence, not title-based deference.
- **Quote snippet**: "procedures are adhered to" - 10 CFR Part 55, operating test requirements (https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-10/chapter-I/part-55)
- **Quote snippet**: "compromises the integrity" - 10 CFR § 55.49, Integrity of examinations and tests (https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-10/chapter-I/part-55)

## High Reliability Organizing (Weick, Sutcliffe, Roberts)

- **Core claim**: Reliability in hazardous work is actively produced through preoccupation with failure, reluctance to simplify, sensitivity to operations, commitment to resilience, and deference to expertise.
- **Use it when**: The work is complex, time-sensitive, and cannot be made safe by documentation alone. The team needs live attention to weak signals, anomalies, and front-line expertise.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A claim that good organizations can safely do anything. HRO practice lowers risk through disciplined attention; it does not erase physical, legal, or architectural limits.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Weick and Sutcliffe's HRO frame is a counterweight to complacency: reliability depends on mindful organizing before and during the unexpected. Source: Weick and Sutcliffe, *Managing the Unexpected*, 3rd ed., Jossey-Bass, 2015.

## Normal Accident Theory (Charles Perrow)

- **Core claim**: In complex, tightly coupled systems, small failures can interact in unexpected ways faster than operators can understand them. The possibility of system accident is a design fact, not merely an operator-quality problem.
- **Use it when**: A proposal assumes that redundancy, training, or expert staffing fully neutralizes complexity and coupling.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Fatalism. The lens should force decoupling, simplification, observability, and bounded blast radius, not resignation.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Perrow argues that interactive complexity and tight coupling make some accidents "normal" in the sense of system-produced rather than freakish. Source: Charles Perrow, *Normal Accidents: Living with High-Risk Technologies*, Princeton University Press, revised ed., 1999.

## Rickover-Style Technical Accountability (Naval Reactors Lineage)

- **Core claim**: Nuclear operations require personal accountability for technical truth, exacting standards, and refusal to reduce a successful program to one easy gimmick.
- **Use it when**: A team wants a slogan, hero, or isolated control to stand in for an integrated safety program.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Personality cult or authoritarian cosplay. The usable lineage is technical rigor, disciplined qualification, and accountable ownership.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Rickover-style standards emphasize integrated technical accountability, qualification, and exacting ownership rather than a single safety gimmick. Source: Theodore Rockwell, *The Rickover Effect: How One Man Made a Difference*, Naval Institute Press, 1992.

## INPO Continuous Improvement and Operating Experience

- **Core claim**: Nuclear operators improve by treating operating experience as shared safety data, not local embarrassment. Learning is a plant function.
- **Use it when**: An incident review, launch review, or migration postmortem is drifting toward blame, secrecy, or "no harm, no foul."
- **Do not misuse it as**: Bureaucratic benchmarking. The point is to change standards, training, and controls based on weak signals and peer learning.
- **Quote snippet**: "highest levels of safety and reliability" - INPO, "Promoting Excellence in Nuclear Operations" (https://www.inpo.info/)
