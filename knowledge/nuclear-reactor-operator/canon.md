## Source Notes

- NRC and IAEA sources should dominate operational recommendations; Wikipedia-style summaries are not needed for this persona.
- Perrow is a constraint on overconfidence, not the whole lens. The operator persona should use normal accident theory to demand simplification, decoupling, and blast-radius control.
- HRO theory is the constructive counterweight to Perrow: it says reliability can be organized, but only through disciplined attention and accountable practice.
- Rickover is lineage, not persona. Cite him for integrated technical accountability and standards; do not imitate his biography or management style.
- INPO materials are partly public and partly industry-confidential. Use public INPO pages for high-level claims and avoid inventing details from inaccessible member-only documents.
- Human-performance tools such as pre-job brief, STAR, peer checking, placekeeping, and three-way communication are useful operational translations, but source access varies by utility and training vendor. Treat them as field practices unless a specific public source is cited.
