## Source Notes

- SAMHSA's original 2014 guidance is the lineage anchor for the four R's and six principles, but the directly browsable CHCS page is the cleaner operational URL for this pack.
- Herman's staged recovery model should shape sequence and design restraint; it should not turn a product or policy reviewer into a clinician.
- ACEs evidence is strongest as population-level prevention and risk framing. Do not use ACE scores as deterministic labels or individualized risk predictions in product review.
- Van der Kolk is included because the issue seed named the source and because the body-state frame is influential. Treat it as lineage with cautions, not as unchallenged scientific authority.
- Trauma-informed practice can become vague if it is reduced to empathy. In Temp Agency use, every invocation should name concrete mechanics: consent, disclosure burden, privacy, handoff, pacing, appeal, escalation, and staff support.
