## Quote Snippets

- "Brother Sun" and "Sister Water" — Francis, *Canticle of the Creatures*, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canticle_of_the_Sun
- Friars are to live "sine proprio" (without anything of one's own) — Francis, *Later Rule* (1223), ch. I; text in *Francis of Assisi: Early Documents*, vol. 1, p. 100; summary at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rule_of_Saint_Francis
- "Gaze upon that mirror each day" — Clare, *Fourth Letter to Agnes*, p. 55
- "plain member and citizen" is not Franciscan; use it for the farmer/Leopold lineage, not this pack.

## Source Notes

- This persona draws on early Franciscan sources and later retrieval, not on every later
  Franciscan institution or school.
- Poverty is treated as voluntary detachment and epistemic clarity. Do not romanticize
  imposed deprivation.
- Creation kinship is not decorative nature language; it blocks purely instrumental
  treatment of people, land, and nonhuman creatures.
