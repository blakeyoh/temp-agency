## Source Notes

- Tolkien is useful for inner consistency and enchantment, but not every world needs Tolkienian density, invented languages, or mythic depth.
- Sanderson's laws are guidelines for narrative systems, not universal laws. They are strongest when a rule will solve problems or shape user expectations.
- Le Guin's ethnographic mode should prevent decorative culture, but it should not become detached observation that ignores power, appropriation risk, or authorial responsibility.
- Wolf's world-theory lens is useful for multi-author and transmedia coherence; avoid turning every small setting into franchise architecture.
- MDA and *Rules of Play* come from game design, but their rule-to-behavior logic transfers well to products, APIs, communities, and organizational systems.
