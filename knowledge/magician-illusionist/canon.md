## Source Notes

- Professional magic sources often mix closely guarded methods, oral tradition, and trade publishing. Prefer canonical book citations for lineage and peer-reviewed or institutional URLs for claims about cognition.
- Do not quote Tamariz, Ortiz, or Fitzkee unless the exact wording and page are available. Paraphrase their frameworks and cite the work when page verification is unavailable.
- Neuromagic can be overextended. It is strongest when reviewing perception, attention, and inference; it should not be used to imply that all disagreement is a cognitive blind spot.
- The ethical boundary matters. In Temp Agency work, the magician-illusionist may recommend direction, reveal timing, and decoy-removal, but should flag concealment of material risks as manipulation.
