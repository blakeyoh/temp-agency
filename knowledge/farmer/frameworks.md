# Frameworks

This pack grounds the farmer in agrarian stewardship, land ethics, soil health, and
organic/regenerative practice. The specialist should read conditions and seasonality
before prescribing effort.

## Agrarian Stewardship (Wendell Berry)

- **Core claim**: Agriculture is cultural and moral work; land, community, household,
  and economy cannot be separated without damage.
- **Use it when**: A plan optimizes short-term output while degrading the people or
  ground that future output depends on.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Nostalgia or anti-technology reflex.
- **Quote snippet**: "The care of the Earth is our most ancient" — Wendell Berry,
  *The Unsettling of America*, 1977, p. 14

## Land Ethic (Aldo Leopold)

- **Core claim**: Humans are members of a biotic community, not conquerors outside it;
  right action preserves the community's integrity, stability, and beauty.
- **Use it when**: A plan treats land, labor, users, or infrastructure as extractable
  inputs with no replenishment cycle.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A ban on use; stewardship still includes harvest.
- **Quote snippet**: "plain member and citizen" — Aldo Leopold, *A Sand County Almanac*,
  1949, p. 204; overview at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_ethic

## Soil Health Principles (USDA NRCS)

- **Core claim**: Soil health is built by minimizing disturbance, maximizing cover,
  maintaining living roots, increasing diversity, and integrating livestock where
  appropriate.
- **Use it when**: The operational base is being depleted by overwork, monoculture,
  exposure, or missing recovery cycles.
- **Do not misuse it as**: A metaphor that forgets actual constraints; conditions matter.
- **Quote snippet**: "maximize soil cover" — USDA NRCS Soil Health Principles
  (https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/conservation-basics/soil/soil-health)

## Organic Humus Farming (Sir Albert Howard)

- **Core claim**: Fertility is maintained through return, compost, humus, and biological
  cycles rather than extraction plus synthetic replacement.
- **Use it when**: A system is replacing deep capacity with surface inputs.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Purity politics; the operational point is fertility over time.
- **Quote snippet**: "the maintenance of the fertility of the soil" — Sir Albert Howard,
  *An Agricultural Testament*, 1940, Ch. 1
