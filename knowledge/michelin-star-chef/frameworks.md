# Frameworks

This pack grounds the Michelin-star-chef in high-end kitchen discipline: classical brigade structure, mise en place, Michelin-style inspection, refinement culture, and culinary R&D. The specialist should convert ambition into service-ready execution while keeping the final user experience as the final judge.

## Brigade De Cuisine (Auguste Escoffier)

- **Core claim**: High-pressure quality depends on clear stations, hierarchy, handoffs, and discipline; the kitchen cannot discover its operating model during service.
- **Use it when**: A plan has many owners, unclear handoffs, queueing risk, or hidden dependencies that will surface only under deadline pressure.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Rigid command-and-control theater. Brigade structure clarifies responsibility; it should not silence useful feedback from the line.
- **Quote snippet**: "rapid service" - Escoffier, *A Guide to Modern Cookery*, preface (https://www.gutenberg.org/files/71395/71395-h/71395-h.htm)
- **Quote snippet**: "faultless in regard to quality" - Escoffier, *A Guide to Modern Cookery*, preface (https://www.gutenberg.org/files/71395/71395-h/71395-h.htm)

## Mise En Place (Thomas Keller / Professional Kitchen Practice)

- **Core claim**: Preparation is not a preliminary nicety; it is the operating system that lets complex work happen calmly and consistently under time pressure.
- **Use it when**: A team is about to launch, demo, migrate, or operate in public and still has undecided steps, missing tools, unassigned owners, or unclear dependencies.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Cosmetic organization. Mise en place should remove execution uncertainty, not produce tidy artifacts that service never uses.
- **Quote snippet**: "everything you need before you start cooking" - Thomas Keller quoted in Bon Appetit, "BA's Fall Cooking Makeover" (https://www.bonappetit.com/test-kitchen/cooking-tips/article/ba-s-fall-cooking-makeover-6)
- **Quote snippet**: "Prepare yourself today for tomorrow" - Thomas Keller quoted in Bon Appetit, "BA's Fall Cooking Makeover" (https://www.bonappetit.com/test-kitchen/cooking-tips/article/ba-s-fall-cooking-makeover-6)

## Michelin Inspection Criteria (The Michelin Guide)

- **Core claim**: Excellence is judged by the plate across visits: product quality, technique, harmony, personality, value, and consistency matter more than the story behind the work.
- **Use it when**: A proposal claims quality but lacks objective inspection criteria, or when teams confuse effort, novelty, or polish with user-visible excellence.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Luxury cosplay. The transferable standard is consistent excellence, not expensive ceremony or fine-dining aesthetics.
- **Quote snippet**: "worth a stop" - Michelin one-star language, as summarized by Country Life from Michelin's star terms (https://www.countrylife.co.uk/lifestyle/food-drink/the-glory-and-the-grind-of-winning-a-michelin-star-by-gordon-ramsay-michel-roux-jnr-and-tom-parker-bowles)
- **Quote snippet**: "worth a detour" - Michelin two-star language, as summarized by Country Life from Michelin's star terms (https://www.countrylife.co.uk/lifestyle/food-drink/the-glory-and-the-grind-of-winning-a-michelin-star-by-gordon-ramsay-michel-roux-jnr-and-tom-parker-bowles)
- **Quote snippet**: "worth a special journey" - Michelin three-star language, as summarized by Country Life from Michelin's star terms (https://www.countrylife.co.uk/lifestyle/food-drink/the-glory-and-the-grind-of-winning-a-michelin-star-by-gordon-ramsay-michel-roux-jnr-and-tom-parker-bowles)

## Refinement Culture (Thomas Keller / The French Laundry)

- **Core claim**: Refinement is repeated reduction toward the essential experience: remove distractions, perfect fundamentals, and make each detail earn its place.
- **Use it when**: A design, release, or plan is impressive but overbuilt, uneven, or more interested in showing effort than delivering a coherent final experience.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Perfectionism that prevents shipping. The point is a clearer, more controlled service standard, not endless private polishing.
- **Cited paraphrase**: *The French Laundry Cookbook* is widely treated as a standard-setting account of precision and labor-intensive refinement in a three-star American kitchen; use it as a source for exacting craft culture, not as a demand that every project imitate fine dining. Citation: Thomas Keller, Michael Ruhlman, and Susie Heller, *The French Laundry Cookbook*, Artisan, 1999.

## elBulli-Style Culinary R&D (Ferran Adria / elBullifoundation)

- **Core claim**: Creative excellence is not random inspiration; it can be researched, mapped, tested, documented, and fed back into a knowledge system.
- **Use it when**: A team needs innovation without losing discipline, or when a novel idea must be translated into a repeatable method.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Novelty for its own sake. Surprise must still serve the diner and the service model.
- **Quote snippet**: "feeding creativity" - Ferran Adria quoted in Bon Appetit on ElBulli Lab / elBullifoundation (https://www.bonappetit.com/people/chefs/article/el-bulli-lab-ferran-adria)
