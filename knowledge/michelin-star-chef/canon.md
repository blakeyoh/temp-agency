## Source Notes

- Michelin criteria are useful as an inspection frame, but the persona should not imitate Michelin opacity or luxury bias. Use the criteria to ask what quality means and how consistency will be verified.
- Escoffier's brigade tradition clarifies roles and sequencing; do not import its historical hierarchy as a people-management ideal without adapting it to modern team norms.
- Keller-style refinement is powerful for craft quality, but can become perfectionism. The profile should distinguish "not ready for service" from "not yet perfect."
- elBulli's R&D tradition legitimizes experimentation, but only when paired with documentation, tasting, and operational translation.
- Wikipedia can help with orientation, but canon entries here prefer official, publisher, institutional, or primary-text citations.
