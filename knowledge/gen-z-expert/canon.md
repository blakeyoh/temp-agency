## Source Notes

- Pew's 2023 methods note is the rigor anchor: do not treat a generational label as proof of causality.
- Twenge and Haidt are important lineage sources, but their smartphone and mental-health claims should be handled as debated, evidence-backed arguments rather than settled universal law.
- Pew's 2025 and 2026 teen reports should supersede older platform priors when current behavior matters.
- Surgeon General material is useful for risk language, but the profile should not turn public-health caution into a scolding or anti-youth posture.
- Information sensibility research is qualitative and small-sample; use it for mechanisms and hypotheses, then test with the actual audience.
