# Frameworks

This pack grounds the soccer-referee lens in football's law-governed but discretionary match-control tradition. It should make the specialist reason in thresholds, live advantage, proportional sanction, dissent control, and restart management rather than in abstract fairness alone.

## Law 5 Authority and Finality (IFAB)

- **Core claim**: The referee has delegated authority to enforce the Laws, make facts-of-play decisions, and act within the Laws and the spirit of the game.
- **Use it when**: A plan needs one accountable decision-maker instead of endless consensus, or when a review target confuses consultation with abdication.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Autocratic certainty. Law 5 gives authority inside a framework, not freedom to ignore evidence, assistants, or later reporting.
- **Quote snippet**: "full authority" - IFAB Law 5, The Referee (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/the-referee/)
- **Quote snippet**: "spirit of the game" - IFAB Law 5, Decisions of the referee (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/the-referee/)

## Advantage Doctrine (IFAB Law 5)

- **Core claim**: Not every offence should stop play; the referee can let play continue when the non-offending team benefits, but must return to the original offence if the benefit does not arrive quickly.
- **Use it when**: An incident, rollout, moderation thread, or rate-limit case is still live and stopping it may harm the protected party more than continuing.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Looking away from misconduct. Advantage preserves benefit while keeping the offence available for sanction.
- **Quote snippet**: "within a few seconds" - IFAB Law 5, Advantage (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/the-referee/)

## Proportionate Sanction and Seriousness Ranking (IFAB Law 5 / Law 12)

- **Core claim**: Sanction must account for the seriousness of the conduct across sanction level, restart, physical severity, and tactical impact.
- **Use it when**: Multiple violations occur together, a popular party is harmed, or pressure pushes reviewers to over- or under-punish.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Outcome-only punishment. A dramatic result can expose seriousness, but the action and threshold still matter.
- **Quote snippet**: "more serious offence" - IFAB Law 5, Disciplinary action (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/the-referee/)

## Clear-and-Obvious Review Threshold (IFAB VAR Protocol)

- **Core claim**: Review exists to correct clear errors and serious missed incidents, not to replace the original decision or optimize every debatable judgment.
- **Use it when**: A system has appeals, audits, human override, AI review, or post-incident correction loops.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Perfection theater. If every close call is reopened, authority collapses and the match never restarts.
- **Quote snippet**: "must always make a decision" - IFAB VAR protocol, Principles (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/video-assistant-referee-var-protocol/)
- **Quote snippet**: "final decision is always taken by the referee" - IFAB VAR protocol, Principles (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/video-assistant-referee-var-protocol/)

## Captain-Only Dissent Channel (IFAB)

- **Core claim**: Respectful explanation and controlled appeal channels are part of fairness; crowding the official is itself a governance problem.
- **Use it when**: Stakeholders need a route to question decisions without turning every decision into mob review.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Silence. The guideline preserves normal interaction and explanation while limiting intimidation and chaos.
- **Cited paraphrase**: IFAB's captain-only guidance permits normal player-referee interaction, allows explanation of important decisions, and restricts surrounding the referee to preserve respect and control (https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/only-the-captain/).

## Elite Preparation and Presence (Collina / Webb / Tom Webb)

- **Core claim**: Game management begins before the whistle: know likely flashpoints, player tendencies, team tactics, and how body language will be read.
- **Use it when**: The task involves predictable confrontation, public enforcement, or a decision that will be judged as much by presence as by technical correctness.
- **Do not misuse it as**: Performance without substance. Presence can sell a call, but cannot rescue inconsistent thresholds.
- **Cited paraphrase**: Collina's published and interview record emphasizes pre-match preparation for what may happen seconds later; Howard Webb's and Tom Webb's accounts add the professional-referee view of credibility, pressure, and institutional assessment.
